about

Think of a document that you would create in a word processor like Microsoft Word or Google Docs. They usually consist of more than one style. They use different font sizes to indicate different sections of the text, like headers, body paragraphs, footers, table of contents, captions, and so on. Unlike humans who can simply look at a document and understand the difference between a heading and a paragraph, computers don’t have that intuition. In order to correctly render a web page, it must be explicitly told what each piece of content is.
